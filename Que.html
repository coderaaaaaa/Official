Questions

Why was the Stamp Act important to the American Revolution?

It both extracted money from Americans without their consent, so both violated the principle of taxation without representation. Colonists rioted against the Stamp Act.

What happened as a result of the Stamp Act?

After the Stamp Act was passed, many colonists protested against it. They didn't want to pay the tax, so groups like the Sons of Liberty formed to fight back. People even attacked the homes of tax collectors. The protests got so big that the British government decided to cancel the Stamp Act the next year.

What did the Stamp Act accomplish?

The Stamp Act was supposed to help the British government make money to pay for soldiers in America. But instead of helping, it made the colonists more upset. It showed them that they needed to stand up for their rights and eventually led to the American Revolution.

How did the colonists respond to the Stamp Act?

The colonists responded to the Stamp Act by organizing protests and writing letters to the British government. They also boycotted British goods, which means they refused to buy them. This united many colonists, and they began to see themselves as one group fighting against British control.

What was the impact of the Stamp Act on British-American relations?

The Stamp Act made British-American relations really bad. It created a lot of anger and distrust between the colonists and the British government. Colonists felt that their rights were being ignored, and this led to more people wanting to break away from Britain and become independent.
